# A Comprehensive Study of Nim Games

This repository contains an academic report on **Nim**, a foundational impartial combinatorial game, focusing on its mathematical structure, complete proofs, diagrams, and important variants. The report is designed to be self-contained and suitable for undergraduate study or a short conference-style paper.

## Report Details

**Title:** A Comprehensive Study of Nim Games: Proofs, Diagrams, and Variants  
**Authors:**  
- Kotha Anshul Reddy (U23AI107)  
- Mihir Hajare (U23AI092)  

**Department:** Artificial Intelligence  
**Institute:** SVNIT, Surat  

## Contents

- Introduction to Nim and impartial games  
- Formal definitions and notation  
- Bouton’s Theorem with full proof  
- Binary representation and nim-sum strategy  
- Illustrative heap and nim-sum diagrams  
- Sprague–Grundy Theorem (theory and examples)  
- Take-and-Break game example  
- Variants of Nim:
  - Fibonacci Nim  
  - Moore’s Nim (Greedy-Move Nim)  
  - Modular Nim  
- Worked example of optimal play  
- Algorithmic complexity and implementation notes  
- Discussion and future directions  

## Objective

The goal of this report is to present a rigorous yet accessible treatment of Nim, combining mathematical proofs with intuitive explanations and visualizations, and to explore how classical results extend to more advanced variants.
